# Customer Acquisition Strategy - TrackerDelivery

## Overview
A comprehensive 4-week customer acquisition strategy targeting 30 paying customers for TrackerDelivery, focused on foreign F&B business owners in Bali.

## Key Metrics & Goals
- **Target**: 30 paying customers in 4 weeks
- **Budget**: $340/month
- **Target Audience**: Foreign F&B owners in Bali
- **Primary Pain Point**: Losing $70-200/day from undetected platform closures

## Core Strategy Channels

### 1. Silent Monitoring + Direct Outreach
**Approach**: Monitor delivery platforms to identify restaurants with issues, then reach out with specific examples
- Identify restaurants with status inconsistencies
- Document specific instances of being "closed" when they should be open
- Direct outreach with proof of lost revenue opportunities
- Immediate value demonstration

### 2. Affiliate Partnership Program
**Approach**: Partner with local business services and consultants
- Partner with business registration services
- Work with local business consultants
- Commission-based referral structure
- Focus on new restaurant openings

### 3. AI Content + Facebook Groups
**Approach**: Educational content marketing in relevant communities
- Create AI-generated content about delivery platform optimization
- Target Facebook groups for expat business owners in Bali
- Share case studies and tips
- Build authority and trust before selling

## Timeline & Milestones

### Week 1-2: Foundation + First 10 Customers
**Setup Phase**:
- Finalize monitoring infrastructure
- Create outreach templates and sequences
- Set up affiliate program structure
- Begin content creation for Facebook groups

**Customer Acquisition**:
- Target: 10 paying customers
- Focus: Direct outreach with silent monitoring data
- Begin affiliate partnerships

### Week 3-4: Scale to 30 Customers
**Scaling Phase**:
- Optimize successful channels from weeks 1-2
- Expand affiliate network
- Increase content marketing frequency
- Refine messaging based on early customer feedback

**Customer Acquisition**:
- Target: Additional 20 customers (total 30)
- Leverage social proof from early customers
- Implement referral incentives

## Target Customer Profile

### Demographics
- **Location**: Bali, Indonesia
- **Business Type**: Foreign-owned F&B establishments
- **Business Size**: Small to medium restaurants/cafes
- **Platform Presence**: Active on GrabFood/GoFood

### Pain Points
- Language barriers preventing manual monitoring
- Time zone differences affecting oversight
- Lost revenue from undetected closures ($70-200/day)
- Lack of reliable staff for platform management

### Value Proposition
- 24/7 automated monitoring
- Immediate alerts for status changes
- Revenue protection and optimization
- English-language support and interface

## Channel-Specific Tactics

### Silent Monitoring + Direct Outreach
1. **Data Collection**: Monitor target restaurants for 48-72 hours
2. **Evidence Gathering**: Document specific instances of missed opportunities
3. **Personalized Outreach**: Send specific examples with revenue impact calculations
4. **Follow-up Sequence**: 3-touch sequence over 1 week

### Affiliate Partnerships
1. **Partner Identification**: Business consultants, legal services, accounting firms
2. **Commission Structure**: 20-30% of first month revenue
3. **Support Materials**: Provide partners with sales materials and training
4. **Performance Tracking**: Monthly partner performance reviews

### AI Content + Facebook Groups
1. **Content Calendar**: 3-5 posts per week across target groups
2. **Value-First Approach**: Educational content before promotional content
3. **Community Building**: Engage authentically in discussions
4. **Conversion Path**: Content → Email List → Sales Conversation

## Risk Mitigation

### Channel Concentration Risk
- Diversify across all three channels simultaneously
- Monitor performance weekly and adjust allocation

### Seasonal/Market Risks
- Focus on established restaurants less affected by seasonality
- Build relationships for long-term retention beyond tourist seasons

### Competition Response
- Maintain unique value proposition (silent monitoring approach)
- Focus on service quality and customer success

## Next Steps & Implementation

### Immediate Actions (Week 1)
1. Set up monitoring infrastructure for silent tracking
2. Create affiliate partner onboarding materials
3. Identify and join target Facebook groups
4. Develop outreach templates and sequences

### Weekly Review Process
- Track customer acquisition by channel
- Adjust budget allocation based on performance
- Refine messaging and tactics based on feedback
- Scale successful approaches, pause underperforming ones
